Designing a blog that keeps the text in charge

A reading interface for a markdown-driven blog should help the reader orient themselves without stealing attention from the article. That requirement naturally leads to a light structure, narrow rules, and a generous main column.

Editorial cadence

The brief asks for a left-side table of contents and a right-side article body. That split works well when the table of contents behaves like a quiet utility rather than a dominant sidebar.

On mobile, removing the table of contents is the right tradeoff. The reading flow matters more than keeping every navigation affordance visible at once.

Spacing and line work

Because the visual language is deliberately minimal, spacing becomes the primary organizing device. The thin borders divide sections softly while the typography carries hierarchy.

  • Headers stay crisp and slightly condensed.
  • Body text gets enough width for comfort but not enough to feel loose.
  • Action surfaces remain compact and secondary.

Comments and discovery

The article page also has to connect outward. Comments support response, while tag and archive discovery keep older writing accessible instead of burying it in chronological lists.
