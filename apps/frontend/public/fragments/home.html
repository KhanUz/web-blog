Overview

A frontend shaped for a writer, a reader, and a lean admin workflow.

Article reading

Two-column article layout with a collapsible table of contents and a strong reading column.

Article operations

A clean management table for publish state, edit, remove, and recall workflows.

Editorial tools

Split-screen markdown editing with instant preview, plus search, tags, comments, and archive views.

Interpretation

The brief calls for visual restraint rather than decorative UI, so the prototype uses thin borders, generous spacing, and black-and-white contrast instead of heavy color.

HTMX handles view swaps and small action feedback, while TypeScript manages behaviors that need client state, especially markdown preview and page-level interaction.

Reading surface

Article detail

Desktop keeps the TOC on the left at roughly one-fifth width. Mobile removes it to keep the text column clean.

Author workflow

Management table

The table keeps name, date, and tags visible, with compact action menus aligned to the right.

Content creation

Markdown editor

Two vertical halves are maintained on desktop and stacked on smaller screens so the editor remains usable everywhere.

Coverage

Pages mapped from the brief

Search and tag discovery, archive browsing, comments, and the about page are represented in the navigation. This keeps the scaffold close to the eventual product without forcing backend data too early.
